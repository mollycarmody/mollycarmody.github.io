Feel the Music Literally

When we think of music or sound, we think of hearing the frequency, the notes, the volume, the beat. The data of sound passes through our ear drums and is translated by our brain into conviceibale bytes of information: lyrics, melodies, harmonies, stories. What if, beyond simply hearing or feeling the vibrations of sound, we could touch sound?

By using ultra haptic technology and mapping sound to physical properties, we can create a representation of sound in physical form. Music becomes a calculated, representative physical "object". So, we can feel the music, literally.

Ultrahaptic Technology

Ultrahaptic Technology uses ultrasound to create 3D shapes and textures that are felt but not seen
